"Vengeance Wont Change The Past"



Batman, whose real name is Bruce Wayne, witnessed the murder of his parents as a child, which left a profound emotional scar on him. His initial motivation for becoming Batman was rooted in a desire for vengeance against those who had taken his parents' lives. However, as he matured and embraced his role as a crime-fighter, Batman came to realize that vengeance alone was not a sustainable or morally justifiable path.

The phrase "vengeance won't change the past" embodies Batman's realization that no amount of revenge or retribution can bring his parents back or undo the tragedy that occurred. Instead, he chooses to focus on preventing similar tragedies from happening to others by dedicating his life to fighting crime and seeking justice. Batman's mission is about making Gotham City a safer place and upholding the law, rather than simply seeking personal vengeance.

"Training hard, Risking your life... what's the difference?"



Kisuke Urahara is a former captain of the Gotei 13 and a highly skilled and mysterious character in the series.

This quote reflects Urahara's philosophy and personality. He is known for his laid-back and enigmatic demeanor, often speaking in a cryptic or philosophical manner. In this particular quote, Urahara is emphasizing the importance of dedication and effort in training.


Urahara's character is known for imparting wisdom and guidance to the main character, Ichigo Kurosaki, and others throughout the series, often through cryptic or thought-provoking statements like this one. It reflects his role as a mentor and a source of knowledge and support for the young heroes in the story.


He's suggesting that when someone is truly committed to their training and striving for improvement, it can become a central and defining aspect of their life. In essence, there is no clear distinction between one's life and their training when they are wholeheartedly pursuing their goals.
